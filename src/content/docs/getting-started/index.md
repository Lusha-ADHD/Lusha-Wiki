Lusha is a mobile game by Dygie designed to support children with ADHD and their families with everyday routines. The app connects real-life actions, such as brushing teeth, preparing a school bag, or working on a specific behavior, with playful progress in an adventure world.

This page gives you a simple path for discovering Lusha without having to read the whole wiki first.

## Who Is Lusha For?

Lusha is mainly designed for children with ADHD and their parents. A parent or trusted adult can support routine planning, while the child experiences the game, completes quests, and receives encouragement.

The wiki can also help professionals understand Lusha's general positioning before directing a family to official information.

## How Lusha Works

Lusha draws on approaches used in ADHD support: psychoeducation, tools inspired by cognitive behavioral therapy, parenting skills, and positive reinforcement. Its purpose is not to replace healthcare professionals, but to help families put practical guidance into daily use.

The app has three broad parts:

- A parent-child routine system. The parent sets 3 to 5 routines in the child's digital agenda, plus one behavioral challenge. When the child completes a real-life task or challenge, the parent validates it in the app.
- A child-facing psychoeducation and emotion/social skills path. A bear guide introduces key ideas, tells stories that reuse those ideas, then invites the child to apply what they learned through quests.
- A parent-facing path inspired by parenting guidance programs, including Barkley-style behavioral management principles, to help structure the environment and support behavior change.

The motivation system is based on a token economy: desired behaviors completed in real life unlock in-game rewards. The child can then continue exploring for a limited play time, usually around 10 to 15 minutes per day, and needs new rewards to keep progressing.

During the first use, take time to complete the onboarding carefully. The answers help track the impact of the game, measure the child's progress over time, and personalize the game experience according to the child's profile, needs, and family context.

## Before You Start

- Use an official source when installing or consulting Lusha.
- Prepare a few simple routines, such as a morning task, an evening task, or a calming action.
- Present Lusha as a support tool and a game, not as a punishment or a test.
- Keep early goals realistic: one small routine followed consistently is better than a long list that is hard to maintain.

## Recommended First Session

1. Explore the app with the child and notice what naturally interests them.
2. Choose one or two concrete routines to connect with the game.
3. Add more routines or one behavioral challenge gradually if the start goes well.
4. Explain clearly when a task is validated and what happens next in Lusha.
5. Review the session together: what felt easy, what was difficult, and what makes the child want to continue.

## Good Practices

Lusha works best when the adult stays consistent and encouraging. In-game rewards should support a real routine, not replace family conversation. If a situation becomes tense, simplify the goals, take a pause, then restart with a more accessible step.

The parent dashboard helps follow the child's progress. It can make it easier to see what works, what needs more support, and which routines should be adapted.
