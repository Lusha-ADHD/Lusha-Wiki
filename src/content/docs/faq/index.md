This FAQ gives quick answers. When a topic needs more context, it links to the most relevant page.

### What is Lusha?

Lusha is a mobile game by Dygie that helps children with ADHD and their families connect real-life routines with playful progress. It can support topics such as everyday tasks, autonomy, emotional regulation, and family organization.

### Does Lusha replace medical care?

No. Lusha should be understood as a support tool and game experience. It does not replace diagnosis, treatment, medical advice, or follow-up from a qualified professional.

### Who is Lusha for?

Lusha is primarily for children and their families. The wiki can also help professionals or visitors understand the project.

### What is the parent's role?

The parent or trusted adult helps choose realistic routines, supports the child, validates real-life tasks, follows progress, and keeps the link between family life and the game experience.

### What approaches does Lusha draw on?

Lusha combines psychoeducation, tools inspired by cognitive behavioral therapy, parenting skills, and a reward system. The parent-facing part includes principles inspired by Barkley-style parenting guidance.

### How do rewards work?

The parent sets routines or a challenge with the child. When the child completes the real-life action, the parent validates it in the app. That validation gives rewards that let the child continue exploring the game for a limited time.

### How should we start?

Start with a small number of clear goals. One or two simple routines are enough for a first experience. The [How to Get Started](/getting-started/) page gives a more detailed path.

### Is this the official wiki?

Yes. This site is intended to be the canonical public wiki for Lusha.

### Which languages are supported?

The initial language set is English, French, German, Italian, Spanish, and Brazilian Portuguese.
